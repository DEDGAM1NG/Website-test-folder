    • The task of rescuing the prncess (main conflict in the book)

  • In the first chapter and part of the book where a couple buccaneers Ringrose Coxon and others are just here to make and steal some riches, the main conflict is the task of saving a Native Indian princess named Maria, entrusted to Captain Ringrose by her grandfather, who had witnessed her abduction by the Spanish. During Part II, they successfully rescue her from Panama and met a buccaneer named Sharp, where she had been held hostage, and Captain Ringrose adds Coxon to his crew. Now, in Part III, we reach the climax and resolution of the pirate's final raid on the Saga of Peru. Some of them are captured and taken to England, while Princess Maria falls in love with Captain Ringrose. This is depicted in Chapter 29 on page 297.
  • Epic battles in the book(side conflicts that happened in the buccaneer pillaging in the book.)
  • In Part I, the pirates seize the Spanish ship La Santísima Trinidad, laden with precious gold and silver, and conduct raids on various Panamanian coastal towns, including Portobelo, Chagres, and Chepo. Part II sees them crossing the Panama Isthmus, reaching the South Sea, and engaging in looting expeditions across islands and mainland towns like Gorgona, Guayaquil, Paita, and Trujillo using native guides like Josesto for the harsh jungles of Puna, all while contending with rival pirate groups such as Captain Davis and Captain Swan. In the climactic Part III, the pirates mount their boldest assault on Lima, the capital of Peru, battling fierce Spanish forces and local militias. Despite suffering significant losses and betrayals, they manage to escape with a substantial fortune, but their journey concludes with some of them facing a sensational trial for piracy upon their return to England.
